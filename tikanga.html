Extra Tikanga to remember

I bet you pressed on this link because you needed extra help, didnt you? Well, don't worry at ALL, because here are some more basic tikanga that isn't even appointed towards being in a marae.

It could be basic things that you want to know about maori traditions, and how they do things. So I encourage you to really try your best in learning and putting tikanga into your actions daily.

  • Remember these 3 basic values. Whakaute, Whakawhanaungatanga, and Whakapapa.

    • These values all carry deep meaning, that will help you in the future looking through a Maori mindset. Whakaute, which is respect, means respecting everyone. Being kind, having empathy for the right people, and staying humble. Whakawhanaungatanga, which is basically building relationships and bonds with people, not only involves communication and bravery, but you have to remember that everyone has different persepectives and ideas. Being Maori, or thinking Maori means perservering even when it's hard. Lastly, the value of Whakapapa. Knowing where you stand, and where you come from. This value plays a big part in terms of identity, and not only memorising your pepeha, but also understanding that your tupuna have fought for the land you say your from. So I strongly believe that these 3 values really do uphold and represent Maori culture.
