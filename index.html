Main Tikanga of marae

All marae have different protocols and rules. To know these rules and make sure you don't enter the marae in a bad state, here are the main tikanga that you should know before even thinking of stepping on a marae. A marae is sacred, and serves primarily for gathering together, for events like funerals, or even just wananga. Ko te wa kei tua, kei roto i o ringa.

  • When you enter the marae, ALWAYS remove your shoes. Tangohia o hu.

  • Asoloutely no kai in the marae unless you have been given permission to eat. Kaua e kai.
  • Do not walk onto the marae without a greeting or consent from the tangata whenua. Tatari ki te karanga.
  • Always respect the kaumatua, for they hold the most wisdom and matauranga, Whakaute ki nga kaumatua.

  • Try your very best to dress formally, as if your ready to korero on the spot. Kakahu okawa.
  • Aim to be on time, whether its a tangihana (funeral) or even just a daily hui. Kia tika te wa.
  • Ensure that your cell phones/devices are on SILENT. You definitely do not want to interupt kaumata when they are speaking. Wahangu to waea.

    Last of all, my biggest tikanga of all would be to be ready, and take every chance you can get. If you know you can mihi or karanga, do it! On Marae they usually leave kaumatua or more experienced people to do so. Therefore, if you get the opportunity to take up any leading roles, do it, and do it with confidence! See it as a privilege to be able to do so.
